## One Remaining Minor Point

The cardinality constraint definition requires the posit type to have **more than one role** (`n > 1`). This silently excludes unary posit types — properties — from having stated cardinality constraints. For properties, something analogous (e.g., "a person has at most one legal name at a time") is a common real-world need. The definition doesn't preclude handling this some other way, but it's worth either noting the exclusion explicitly ("properties, having a single role, are not addressed by this definition; a natural extension is left as future work") or relaxing the constraint to n ≥ 1. As it stands, a careful reader will notice the restriction and wonder if it's intentional.

That said, this is a minor gap in an otherwise solid formalization section, and it could easily be resolved with a sentence. It wouldn't block publication.
